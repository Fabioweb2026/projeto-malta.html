Gastronomy & Architecture

Maltese culture is deeply integrated into its unique culinary style and magnificent buildings. Baroque features meet massive limestone ramparts engineered centuries ago by the legendary Knights of St. John.

Traditional Mediterranean Flavors

The local food scene presents a delicious fusion of Sicilian and Arabic influences. You must sample Pastizzi, flaky pastries filled with warm ricotta cheese or mushy peas. Other local culinary specialties include slow-cooked rabbit stew (Fenkata), savory Ftira bread, and fresh olive oil appetizers.

Giorgio's Cafe

A premier and essential landmark in Malta's vibrant cafe culture. Located beautifully on the lively Sliema Promenade, it is the absolute best spot to relax with an authentic Mediterranean espresso, traditional sweet pastries, and a spectacular waterfront breeze.

📍 Address: 19, Sliema Promenade, Sliema, Malta
📌 Reference Point: Situated right on the main Strand waterfront walkway looking directly across the harbor towards Valletta.

Delicious Menu Options

Premium dishes crafted with fresh savory ingredients, fine local cheeses, and traditional Mediterranean culinary styles.

Outdoor Terrace Lounge

Relax under the iconic dark blue umbrellas along the promenade, enjoying the ocean breeze and urban movement.

Historical Architectural Icons

Malta's structures are highly detailed masterpieces built out of regional golden globe globigerina limestone.

St. John's Co-Cathedral

A high baroque masterpiece located in Valletta. Features breathtaking gold-carved walls, magnificent hand-painted ceiling frescoes, and historic marble tombs of the Knights of St. John.
